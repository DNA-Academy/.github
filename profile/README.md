### Learning model

DNA Academy programs combine three main components:

1. **Theoretical sessions (online)**  
   - Core concepts in genomics, molecular biology and bioinformatics  
   - Case studies based on up-to-date literature  
   - Live lectures + recordings for later access  

2. **Genomic data analysis (online, hands-on)**  
   - Step-by-step workflows on Google Colab and similar tools  
   - NGS data formats, QC, alignment, variant analysis, microbiome/metagenomics, single-cell etc.  
   - Participants run all commands on their own computers  

3. **Wet-lab applications (onsite, selected programs)**  
   - DNA/RNA isolation, PCR, library preparation  
   - 3rd generation sequencing (especially Oxford Nanopore)  
   - Real samples and field-driven experiments

### How to use this GitHub organization as a participant?

1. **Find your module repo**  
   Look for repositories like `dnaacademy-bif101-demo`, `dnaacademy-dna101`, etc.

2. **Open the notebooks**  
   Inside each repo, go to the `notebooks/` folder and open the `.ipynb` files.  
   Use the **â€œOpen in Colabâ€** button whenever available.

3. **Follow along during live sessions**  
   During the online classes, run the notebooks step by step with the instructor,  
   filling in scaffold/TODO cells and saving your own copy.

4. **Keep your work**  
   You can fork the repo or save a copy in your Google Drive  
   to build your own portfolio of genomic data analysis workflows.

---

### Who are these materials for?

- **Students & early-career researchers**  
  who want a strong foundation in genomics & bioinformatics  

- **Academics & lab groups**  
  aiming to integrate NGS/long-read, eDNA, microbiome or single-cell analysis into their projects  

- **Industry & R&D teams**  
  looking for practical, field-driven genomics & bioinformatics capacity building
